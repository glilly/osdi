TABLE OF INTERACTIONS WITH

BRADYCARIA INDUCING DRUGS

Numerous medications can bring about bradycardia. This is the case especially for the Class 1a antiarrhythmics, the beta-blockers, some Class III antiarrhythmics, some calcium channel blockers, digoxin, pilocarpine, anticholinesterics, … etc.

From the French ANSM drug interactions document of September 2016, p. 45

Increase of the bradycardia inducing effects which can have fatal consequences. The beta-blocking agents are even more at risk since they inhibit the adrenergic compensation mechanisms
