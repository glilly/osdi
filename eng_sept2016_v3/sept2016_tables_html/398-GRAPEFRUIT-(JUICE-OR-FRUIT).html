TABLE OF INTERACTIONS WITH

GRAPEFRUIT (JUICE OR FRUIT)

Grapefruit juice can increase the bioavailability of several medications, especially certain immunosuppressants (ciclosporin, tacrolimus, sirolimus), the dihydropyridines, certain statins and cisapride. The clinical consequences of these interactions are variable and depend on numerous factors such as individual patient susceptibility, the nature as well as the quantity of the grapefruit juice consumed, the fact of simultaneous doses, that act as an inducing factor, and the therapeutic margin of the medication taken with it. As a result, it is recommended that patients be advised not to consume grapefruit juice where there is treatment with these medications, especially if simvastatin, whose concentrations can be considerably increased, is involved.
