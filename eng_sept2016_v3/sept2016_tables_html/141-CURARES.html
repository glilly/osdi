TABLE OF INTERACTIONS WITH

CURARES

Certain substances can modify the intensity and/or the duration of the effect of the non depolarizing curares.

The medications that tend to increase the effects of the action of the non depolarizing curares are:

--volatile anesthetics such as isoflurane, enflurane, desflurane, sevoflurane, or halothane

--local anesthetics

--some antibiotics (aminoglycosides, polymixines, lincosonides)

--magnesium sulfate (IV)

--calcium antagonists

--medications that increase or reveal a myasthenic syndrome, such as the fluorquinolones, telithromycin, erythromycin IV, quinidine, or lithium.

The medications that tend to decrease the action of the non depolarizing curares are:

--phenytion or carbamazepine when administered over a long period of time

The use of curares with patients receiving injectable corticoids exposes them to the risk of myopathy, possibly of long duration.

In principle, monitoring maintained until complete decurarisation permits avoiding the incidence of an interaction. However, an unanticipated recurarisation could occur, in the case of a post operative antibiotic umbrella with an aminoglycoside, for example.

From the French ANSM drug interactions document of September 2016, p. 65
