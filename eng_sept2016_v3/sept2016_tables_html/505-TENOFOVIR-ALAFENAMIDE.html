TABLE OF INTERACTIONS WITH

TENOFOVIR ALAFENAMIDE

From the French ANSM drug interactions document of

September 2016, pp. 213-214

TENOFOVIR ALAFENAMIDE

RxNorm: 1721603

ATC: not found

BOCEPREVIR

RxNorm: 1102129

ATC: J05AE12

Risk of lower effectiveness of the tenofovir alafenamide due to decrease of its intracellular activity by the boceprevir

Not recommended

TENOFOVIR ALAFENAMIDE

RxNorm: 1721603

ATC: not found

CICLOSPORIN

RxNorm: 3008

ATC:

L04AD01 S01XA18

Increase of the plasma concentrations of the tenofovir alafenamide due to increase of its absorption

Precaution for use

In case of co-administration with the ciclosporine, the dose of tenofovir alafenamide must be limited to 10 mg per day.

TENOFOVIR ALAFENAMIDE

RxNorm: 1721603

ATC: not found

COBICISTAT

RxNorm: 1306284

ATC: VO3AX03

With the atazanavir, the darunavir or the lopinavir boosted with cobicistat, increase of the plasma concentrations of the tenofovir alafenamide due to increase of its absorption

Precaution for use

In case of co-administration, the dose of tenofovir alafenamide must be limited to 10 mg per day. The administration with the other protease inhibitors of HIV has not been studied.

TENOFOVIR ALAFENAMIDE

RxNorm: 1721603

ATC: not found

ENZYME INDUCERS

CLASS CODE:

N03-J05-001

Decrease of the plasma concentrations of the tenofovir alafenamide due to decrease of its absorption by the inducer

Not recommended

If the administration of these medications together cannot be avoided, clinical monitoring during the administration and 1 to2 weeks after the inducer is stopped.

TENOFOVIR ALAFENAMIDE

RxNorm: 1721603

ATC: not found

RITONAVIR BOOSTED PROTEASE INHIBITORS

CLASS CODE: J05AE-002

With the atazanavir, the darunavir or the lopinavir, increase of the plasma concentrations of the tenofovir alafenamide due to increase of its absorption

Precaution for use

If these are administered together, the dose of tenofovir alafenamide must be limited to 10 mg per day. Administration with the other protease inhibitors of HIV has not been studied.
