MEDICATIONS AT THE ORIGIN OF A SEROTONIN SYNDROME

Some overdoses or some medications can lead to a serotonin syndrome that justifies an immediate end of the treatment

These medications are essentially represented by

--linezolide

--methylene blue

--St Johns wort

--pethidine and tramadol

--most antidepressants

--selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors

--some tricyclic antidepressants (clomipramine, amitrypyline, imipramine, trimipramine)

--mixed (venlafaxine, milnacipran, sibutramine)

--with indications other than for depressioin : atomoxetine, duloxetine, oxitriptan)

--the MAOIs, essentially the non selectives, but also the selective MAOI-As

Serotonin syndrome manifests by the appearance (possibly abrupt), simultaneous or sequential, of a group of symptoms that can require hospitalization, even exceptionally, lead to death.

These symptoms can be of the order of:

--digestive (diarrhea)

--neuropsychic (agitation, confusion, hypomania)

--motor (myoclonia, trembling, hyperreflexia, rigidity, hyperactivity)

--vegetative (blood pressure variations, tachycardia, chills, hyperthermia, sweats. possibly coma).

The strict respect of recommended dosages constitutes an essential factor in the prevention of this syndrome.

The level of risk is very variable depending on the interactions. The range is serious, even fatal, especially with the non selective MAOIs (consult the contraindicated interactions in the Thesaurus)
