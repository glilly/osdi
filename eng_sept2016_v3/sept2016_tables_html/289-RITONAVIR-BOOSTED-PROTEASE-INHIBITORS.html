TABLE OF INTERACTIONS WITH

RITONAVIR BOOSTED PROTEASE INHIBITORS

Ritonavir is now used in 100mg doses 2 times a day when combined with another protease inhibitor, in order to increase very significantly the bioaccessibility of the protease inhibitor associated with it (or boosted), which permits increasing in this way the effectiveness of the antiretroviral treatment. With regard to this therapeutic plan, each protease inhibitor acquires in this way powerful properties of inhibition of the CYP3A4 that ritonavir possesses, and this is so even when the latter is given in doses 6 times less than in previous years of its use. In practice, this means considering all the protease inhibitors as enzymatic inhibitors of comparable strength. Consequently, the interactions common to this classification, for the most part, flow from this property.

From the French ANSM drug interactions document of

September 2016, pp. 123-125

It is recommended that that the protease inhibitor be administered as far from the afatinib as possible, preferably at an interval of 6 to 12 hours from the dose of afatinib.
