TABLE OF INTERACTIONS WITH

SEDATIVE MEDICATIONS

It is necessary to take into account the fact that numerous medications or substances can multiply their effects of central nervous system depression and contribute to diminished alertness. It is a question of morphine derivatives (analgesics, antitussives, and substitution treatments), neuroleptics, barbiturates, benzodiazepines, anxiolytics other than benzodiazepines (for example meprobamate), hypnotics, sedative antidepressants (amitriptyline, doxepin, mianserin, mirtazapine, trimipramine), H1 sedative antihistamines, central antihypertensives, baclofen, and thalidomide.

From the French ANSM drug interactions document of September 2016, p. 149
