MEDICATIONS AT THE ORIGIN OF ORTHOSTATIC HYPOTENSION

Aside from the antihypertensives, numerous medications can lead to orthostatic hypotension. This is especially the case with nitrate derivatives, inhibitors of type 5 phospodiesterase, alpha blockers for urological use, tricyclic antidepressants, and phenothiazinic neuroleptics, dopaminergic agonists, and levodopa. Their combined use thus risks increasing the frequency and intensity of this undesirable effect. Consult the interactions specific to each class, with the corresponding level of constraint.
