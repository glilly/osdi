ACETYLCHOLINESTERASE INHIBITORS

+ ATROPINIC MEDICATIONS

It is necessary to take into account the risk of administering an atropinic medication (tricyclic antidepressants, neuroleptics, phenothiazines, antispasmotics, some H1-antihistamines …) to a patient also taking an anticholinasterase. Aside from the possible decrease of the therapeutic effect of the latter, the abrupt interruption of treatment with an atopinic exposes the patient to the risk of uncovering the muscarinic effects of the parasympathomimetic with symptoms of the “cholinergic crisis” type, which can manifest especially with convulsions.

+OTHER ANTI-CHOLINESTERASE MEDICATIONS

It is necessary to take into account the administration of an anticholinasterase, given for a diagnosis such as for myasthenia or intestinal atonia, with another anticholinsaterase given for Alzheimers, due to the risks of additive undesirable effects of the cholinergic type, especially digestive ones.
