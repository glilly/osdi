PLATELET AGGREGATION INHIBITORS

Several substances are implicated in the interactions, by reason of their platelet inhibitor properties.

The use of several platelet aggregation inhibitors increases the risk of bleeding, as does their use with heparins or related molecules, with oral anticoagulants, and with thrombolytics, and must be taken into account in maintaining regular clinical monitoring.

For acetylsalicylic acid, refer to the interactions specific to it.
