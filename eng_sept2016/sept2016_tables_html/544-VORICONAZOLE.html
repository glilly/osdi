VORICONAZOLE

RxNorm: 121243

ATC: J02AC03

AMIODARONE

RxNorm: 703

ATC: C01BD01

Increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias, especially of torsades de pointes, due to possible decrease of the metabolism of the amiodarone.

Precaution for use

Clinical monitoring and EKG, and possible adjustment of the dosage of the amiodarone.

VORICONAZOLE

RxNorm: 121243

ATC: J02AC03

ANTICONVULSANT ENZYME INDUCERS

CLASS CODE:

N03A-001

--for carbamazepine, phenobarbital, primidone:

Risk of reduction of the effectiveness of the voriconazole due to increase of its hepatic metabolism by the inducer

--for phenytoin, fosphenytoin:

Large decrease of the plasma concentrations of the voriconazole with risk of loss of effectiveness, due to increase of its hepatic metabolism by the phenytoin, for one thing; and increase of the plasma concentrations of the phenytoin due to decrease of its hepatic metabolism by the voriconazole, for another.

Contraindication

--for carbamazepine, phenobarbital, primidone

Not recommended

--for phenytoin, fosphenytoin

If the administration of these medications together cannot be avoided, strict clinical monitoring, plasma concentration dosage of phenytoin and possible adjustment of the dosages during the administration of these substances together and after the voriconazole is stopped.

VORICONAZOLE

RxNorm: 121243

ATC: J02AC03

VITAMIN K ANTAGONISTS

CLASS CODE:

B01AA-001

Increase of the effect of the vitamin K antagonist and of the risk of hemorrhage due to decrease of its hepatic metabolism

Precaution for use

More frequent testing of the INR. Possible adjustment of the dosage of the vitamin K antagonist during the treatment with voriconazole and a week after it is stopped.

IMMUNOSUPPRESSANTS

CLASS CODE:

L04A

Increase of the blood concentrations of the immunosuppressant due to inhibition of its hepatic metabolism by the voriconazole.

Not recommended

Precaution for use

--with the ciclosporin and the tacrolimus:

blood concentration dosage of the immunosuppressant, testing of the renal function, and adjustment of the dosage during the administration of the medications together and after it is stopped.
